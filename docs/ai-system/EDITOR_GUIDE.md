## Introduction

### Welcome, Content Editors!

As a Content Editor at CoinDaily, you work alongside our AI system to ensure every article meets our high-quality standards. This guide will help you:

- **Review AI-generated content** efficiently
- **Assess quality** using our criteria
- **Provide effective feedback** to improve AI performance
- **Handle revisions** smoothly
- **Optimize for SEO** and user engagement
- **Maintain consistency** across all content

### Your Role

You are the **quality gatekeeper** between AI-generated content and publication. Your responsibilities:

1. Review articles assigned to you for quality and accuracy
2. Approve high-quality content for publication
3. Request revisions for content that needs improvement
4. Provide feedback to help AI learn and improve
5. Ensure brand voice and style consistency
6. Verify factual accuracy and compliance

## Quality Assessment Criteria

### The 7 Quality Metrics

Every article is evaluated on 7 key metrics. Your job is to verify the AI's assessment and make the final call.

#### 1. Grammar & Language (Weight: 15%)

**What to Check**:
- âœ… **Spelling** - Zero typos acceptable
- âœ… **Grammar** - Proper sentence structure
- âœ… **Punctuation** - Correct usage throughout
- âœ… **Consistency** - US English (color, not colour)
- âœ… **Clarity** - Clear, concise sentences

**Target Score**: > 0.90

**Common Issues**:
- Inconsistent tenses
- Run-on sentences
- Missing articles (a, an, the)
- Comma splices

**Action**:
- **Score > 0.90**: Approve (if other metrics good)
- **Score 0.85-0.90**: Quick edits, then approve
- **Score < 0.85**: Request revision with specific grammar notes

**Example Feedback**:
```
Grammar issues identified:
- Paragraph 3: "The market are rising" â†’ "The market is rising"
- Paragraph 5: Run-on sentence, needs splitting
- Throughout: Inconsistent use of "cryptocurrency" vs "crypto currency"
```

#### 2. Factual Accuracy (Weight: 25%)

**What to Check**:
- âœ… **Data accuracy** - Prices, dates, statistics correct
- âœ… **Claims verified** - Can you verify the facts?
- âœ… **Source citations** - Are sources credible?
- âœ… **Up-to-date info** - Is information current?
- âœ… **No misinformation** - Double-check controversial claims

**Target Score**: > 0.90

**How to Verify**:
1. Check cryptocurrency prices against CoinGecko/CoinMarketCap
2. Verify dates and events through news sources
3. Cross-reference statistics with original sources
4. Check blockchain data on explorers
5. Verify company announcements on official channels

**Action**:
- **All facts verified**: Approve
- **Minor inaccuracies**: Edit and approve
- **Major errors**: Request revision with corrections

**Example Feedback**:
```
Factual accuracy issues:
- Bitcoin price listed as $67,000 but current price is $65,432 (Update)
- Article states "ETF approved on January 10" but actual date was January 11
- Source link broken for SEC announcement (Need valid source)
```

#### 3. Readability (Weight: 15%)

**What to Check**:
- âœ… **Reading level** - Appropriate for target audience
- âœ… **Sentence length** - Not too long (< 25 words average)
- âœ… **Paragraph length** - Digestible chunks (< 150 words)
- âœ… **Flow** - Logical progression of ideas
- âœ… **Engagement** - Interesting and engaging tone

**Target Score**: > 0.85 (Flesch Reading Ease: 60-80)

**Tools Available**:
- Readability score shown in dashboard
- Highlight long sentences (click "Show Readability")
- Paragraph length indicator

**Action**:
- **Score > 0.85**: Approve
- **Score 0.75-0.85**: Consider minor edits
- **Score < 0.75**: Request simplification

**Example Feedback**:
```
Readability improvements needed:
- Paragraph 2 is too long (240 words) - suggest breaking into 2 paragraphs
- Multiple sentences exceed 30 words - simplify for better flow
- Technical jargon needs explanation (define "smart contract" for general audience)
```

#### 4. SEO Optimization (Weight: 15%)

**What to Check**:
- âœ… **Title tag** - Present, 50-60 characters, includes keyword
- âœ… **Meta description** - Present, 150-160 characters, compelling
- âœ… **Keywords** - 3-5 primary keywords naturally integrated
- âœ… **Headings** - H2/H3 structure with keywords
- âœ… **Internal links** - At least 3 relevant internal links
- âœ… **External links** - Credible sources cited
- âœ… **Image alt text** - Descriptive and keyword-rich

**Target Score**: > 0.80

**Quick SEO Checklist**:

**Action**:

- **Score < 0.75**: Request SEO revision

**Example Feedback**:
```

- Title too long (72 chars) - shorten to "Bitcoin ETF Approval: Market Impact"
- Missing meta description - suggest: "SEC approves Bitcoin spot ETFs..."
```

## Providing Feedback

### Why Feedback Matters

Your feedback helps the AI learn and improve. The more specific and actionable your feedback, the better future content will be.

### Types of Feedback

**1. Approval Feedback** (Optional but helpful)
```
Example: "Excellent analysis with accurate data and 
well-structured arguments. SEO implementation is 
particularly strong. This is the quality standard 
we're aiming for."

Impact: AI learns what "excellent" looks like
```

**2. Revision Feedback** (Required)
```
Example: "Good foundation but needs improvement:
1. Grammar: Fix comma splices in paragraphs 2 and 5
2. Accuracy: Update Bitcoin price to current market price
3. SEO: Add 2-3 internal links to related articles
4. Completeness: Add conclusion paragraph summarizing key points"

Impact: AI knows exactly what to fix
```

**3. Rejection Feedback** (Required)
```
Example: "Article does not meet quality standards:
- Multiple factual errors (see highlighted sections)
- Poor readability (Flesch score: 42)
- Missing key context about African market impact
- Off-brand tone (too technical, not accessible)

Suggest: Complete rewrite with focus on clarity 
and African market perspective."

Impact: AI understands major issues and starts fresh
```

### Feedback Best Practices

**DO**:
- âœ… Be specific (cite paragraph numbers, examples)
- âœ… Be constructive (explain how to improve)
- âœ… Be clear (use simple, direct language)
- âœ… Provide examples (show correct vs incorrect)
- âœ… Prioritize issues (most important first)

**DON'T**:
- âŒ Be vague ("This isn't good enough")
- âŒ Be rude ("This is terrible")
- âŒ Provide contradictory feedback
- âŒ Omit important context
- âŒ Ignore the feedback template

**End of Shift**

### Quality vs Speed

**Target Metrics**:
- **Review Time**: 10-15 minutes per article
- **Approval Rate**: 70-80% (first review)
- **Revision Rate**: 15-25%
- **Rejection Rate**: < 5%

**Balance Quality and Speed**:
```
âœ“ DO:
  - Use keyboard shortcuts for efficiency

  - Focus on high-impact issues
  - Trust the AI quality scores (within reason)
  - Approve quickly when quality is clearly high

âœ— DON'T:

  - Ignore quality metrics
  - Approve without reading
```
